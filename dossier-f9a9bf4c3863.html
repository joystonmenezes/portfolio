Strategy & positioning

One identity, one spine, outreach-first. This is the whole plan in a paragraph.

The plan in one line
Apply as a computational social scientist to iSchool / social-computing / communication programs, with digital mental health as the spine and misinformation as the strong second, weighted toward California — and do professor outreach now (summer is the best window).
Why you're competitive
  • Production-grade data engineering — million-scale multimodal scraping & pipelines (TikTok / X).
  • Applied ML/LLM for meaningful constructs — Bigorexia harm/recovery classifiers; BERTopic election discourse.
  • A lived ethics & impact story — USC homelessness research, NSS India, Tijuana & Colombia.
  • USC ISI pedigree — Luceri, Ferrara, Lerman as references.
The four positioning fixes
  • One field — computational social science, not CS + Communication + Cognitive Science at once.
  • One spine — lead with digital mental health (most distinctive, least crowded).
  • Custom fit — a unique, paper-specific paragraph per school; never recycled.
  • Outreach-first — email professors before applying; apply where they reply warmly.
Honest geography note
California is strongest for your misinformation / CSS vein (Stanford, UC Davis, Berkeley, UC Irvine). Your mental-health spine mostly lives out of state (Minnesota, UIUC, Georgia Tech) — so keep a few out-of-state mental-health applications so that spine survives.

  • One thing you did, with a number.
  • One ask: recruiting for Fall 2027?

05

Statement of purpose

New opening — no "Panta Rhei," lead with your own finding. Mental health is the spine; a custom fit paragraph per school.

The new opening

"When I trained classifiers to flag harmful 'Bigorexia' content across millions of TikTok videos, the hardest cases were not the obviously dangerous ones — they were the posts where harm and recovery lived in the same caption, the same aesthetic, the same community. That ambiguity convinced me that detecting harmful content is not only an engineering problem; it is a computational social science problem that requires modeling people, context, and meaning at scale."

Structure & the fit-paragraph bank
  • 1 · Hook — the Bigorexia finding above.
  • 2 · Identity — "a computer scientist who became a computational social scientist."
  • 3 · Mental-health thread (Lerman) — taxonomy, LLaMA/Gemini classifiers, the open question.
  • 4 · Information-integrity thread (HUMANS) — 10M comments, BERTopic, the dataset, the measurement question.
  • 5 · Ethics throughline — homelessness, NSS, Tijuana/Colombia → why you study vulnerable communities carefully.
  • 6 · Custom fit paragraph — name 1–2 professors + a specific paper + one concrete project.
  • 7 · Future + goal — causal inference, prebunking, real-time intervention; research scientist / professor.
